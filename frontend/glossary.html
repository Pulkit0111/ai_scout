AI Glossary

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Computer systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as understanding language, recognizing images, or making decisions.

Example: Siri and Alexa are AI assistants that understand and respond to voice commands.

Large Language Model (LLM)

An AI system trained on massive amounts of text data that can understand and generate human-like text. These models power chatbots like ChatGPT and Claude.

Example: ChatGPT is an LLM that can write essays, answer questions, and have conversations.

Machine Learning (ML)

A type of AI where computers learn from examples rather than being explicitly programmed. The more data they see, the better they become.

Example: Netflix learning what movies you like based on your watching history.

Neural Network

A computer system loosely inspired by the human brain, made up of interconnected nodes (like neurons) that work together to recognize patterns and make decisions.

Example: The technology behind face recognition in your phone's camera.

GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer)

A family of AI models created by OpenAI that can generate human-like text. The technology behind ChatGPT.

Example: GPT-4 is the latest version, more powerful and accurate than previous versions.

Prompt

The text you type into an AI system to get a response. Good prompts lead to better answers.

Example: "Write a poem about cats" is a prompt for ChatGPT.

Training Data

The information (text, images, etc.) that AI systems learn from. More and better data usually means better AI.

Example: ChatGPT was trained on books, websites, and articles from the internet.

Transformer

A type of neural network architecture particularly good at understanding language and context. Most modern AI chatbots use transformers.

Example: The "T" in GPT stands for Transformer.

Fine-tuning

Taking an existing AI model and training it further on specific data to make it better at a particular task.

Example: Taking ChatGPT and fine-tuning it on medical data to make a medical AI assistant.

Multimodal AI

AI that can understand and work with multiple types of data like text, images, and audio together.

Example: GPT-4 can analyze both text and images in the same conversation.

Generative AI

AI that can create new content like text, images, music, or code rather than just analyzing existing content.

Example: DALL-E generates images from text descriptions, Midjourney creates artwork.

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

A technique where AI looks up relevant information from a database before generating a response, making answers more accurate and up-to-date.

Example: An AI assistant that searches your company documents before answering questions.

Token

A piece of text that AI models process. Usually a word or part of a word. AI usage is often measured in tokens.

Example: The sentence "Hello world" is about 2-3 tokens.

Hallucination

When an AI confidently provides incorrect information or makes up facts. A known limitation of current AI systems.

Example: ChatGPT inventing a fake research paper that doesn't exist.

AI Agent

An AI system that can take actions on your behalf, like booking appointments, sending emails, or completing tasks.

Example: An AI that can automatically respond to customer service emails.
